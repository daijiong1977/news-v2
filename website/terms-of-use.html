1. What's the site's content

  • Source articles are © their original publishers (PBS NewsHour, BBC, NPR, Al Jazeera, Smithsonian, ScienceDaily, NG Kids, etc.). The site uses them under standard RSS / fair-use terms, with attribution.
  • Derivative content — reading-level rewrites (Sprout / Tree), quizzes, background reads, article structures, Chinese summaries, hero imagery selections — is the original work of the kidsnews team. Copyright © 2026 kidsnews team. All rights reserved.

2. Personal use is welcome

  • Read, share with your family, and use for classroom learning. That's why we're here.
  • Educators may share articles with students; please link back to news.6ray.com rather than re-host the content.

3. Automated access

Our robots.txt is the canonical policy for crawlers. In short:

  • Search engines and social-preview crawlers are welcome — we want families to find us and share cards to render.
  • AI-training crawlers are opted out. Specifically GPTBot, ClaudeBot, anthropic-ai, PerplexityBot, CCBot, Google-Extended, Applebot-Extended, Bytespider, Amazonbot, cohere-ai, Meta-ExternalAgent, and others enumerated in the file. We don't consent to our derivative content training models.
  • Other automated collection — scraping, mirroring, content harvesting — requires express written permission. Bots that ignore robots.txt are misbehaving and we reserve the right to rate-limit, IP-deny, or block them at the edge.

4. Account / kid identity

  • The site uses an anonymous client_id stored in localStorage to track reading progress. Magic-link sign-in lets a kid carry that progress across devices via email.
  • We don't sell data and don't show ads. Reading events sync to a Supabase database under our control, used only for the reading-streak + parent-dashboard features.
  • Parents can request deletion at any time — see contact below.

5. Disclaimers

  • The site is provided "as is" without warranty. Source articles may be incorrect, outdated, or biased — we curate but don't fact-check every claim.
  • Quizzes, background reads, and Article-Structure cards are produced by AI rewriters and reviewed by our team. They occasionally err.
