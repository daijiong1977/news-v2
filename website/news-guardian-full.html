Selfie Snaps Cause $600,000 Fighter Jet Crash

Imagine you're a fighter pilot flying a multi-million-dollar jet. You pull out your phone to snap a selfie for your last flight. That's exactly what happened in South Korea in 2021—and it ended with two F-15K jets crashing into each other, causing about $600,000 in damage. The South Korean air force just released a report blaming the pilots, and one has been ordered to pay back some of the repair costs.

The accident happened in December 2021 near the city of Daegu. Two F-15K fighter jets were flying in formation—that means they were flying close together in a precise pattern. The wingman pilot (the one flying behind and to the side) wanted to record images because it was his final flight with the unit. Without asking for permission, he sharply climbed and banked the aircraft—tilting it sideways—to get a better camera angle. Meanwhile, the lead pilot was also filming video from the front jet.

As the two planes got too close, both crews tried to swerve away. But it was too late. The wingman's tail slammed into the lead jet's wing, tearing a chunk off. The damage cost about 880 million won (that's $600,000) to fix. Amazingly, no one was hurt. The South Korean Board of Audit and Inspection—a government watchdog—said the wingman was mostly to blame. But they also criticized the air force for not having strict rules about in-flight filming back then.

Different people see this differently. The air force apologized publicly, saying, "We sincerely apologize to the public for the concern caused by the accident." The wingman pilot was suspended from flying, got severe punishment, and has since left the military. He also has to repay about a tenth of the repair costs—roughly $60,000. Some people think that's fair; others say the air force should have had better rules to prevent this in the first place.
